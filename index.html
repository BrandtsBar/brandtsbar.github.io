The Old Fashioned
Bourbon · Daily Signature
Woodford Reserve with house-made coriander red pepper syrup & orange
Ingredients
  • 3 ozWoodford Reserve Bourbon
  • ⅜ ozCoriander Red Pepper Simple Syrup
  • 3 dashesAngostura Bitters
  • 1Large single ice cube
  • 1Orange peel, for garnish
Method
  1. 1
    Place large ice cube in a rocks glass.
  2. 2
    Add syrup and bitters to mixing glass. Add Woodford. Fill with ice.
  3. 3
    Stir slowly and deliberately for 30 seconds. Do NOT shake.
    Stirring keeps it crystal clear.
  4. 4
    Strain over the large ice cube.
  5. 5
    Hold orange peel skin-side down, twist firmly to express oils over the drink. Run around rim, drop in.
    The expressed oils are the magic.

Stirred, never shaken — shaking clouds the drink.

Large cube chills slowly and controls dilution perfectly.

Syrup recipe in the House Syrups tab.

The Brandt Manhattan
Rye Whiskey
Bold and classic — the crown jewel of Brandt's Bar
Ingredients
  • 3 ozBulleit Rye Whiskey
  • 1 ozSweet Vermouth
  • 2 dashesAngostura Bitters
  • 3 dashesPeychaud's Bitters
  • 3Luxardo Cherries, garnish
Method
  1. 1
    Chill a coupe or martini glass in the freezer at least 10 minutes.
  2. 2
    Add ice to mixing glass. Pour in Bulleit Rye, sweet vermouth, Angostura, and Peychaud's bitters.
  3. 3
    Stir slowly for 30 seconds. Do NOT shake.
    Stirring keeps it crystal clear.
  4. 4
    Strain into chilled coupe glass.
  5. 5
    Skewer 3 Luxardo cherries on a cocktail pick. Rest across the rim.

Use Carpano Antica or Cocchi di Torino for sweet vermouth — cheap vermouth will ruin it.

Luxardo cherries are non-negotiable. No maraschino cherries at Brandt's Bar.

Served up — no ice in the glass.

Marilyn's Rosemary Lemon Martini
Vodka
Elegant, aromatic, and highly requested at Brandt's Bar
Ingredients
  • 3 ozTito's Vodka
  • ¾ ozFresh lemon juice
  • ½ ozRosemary Simple Syrup
  • 1Fresh rosemary sprig, garnish
  • 1Lemon twist, garnish
Method
  1. 1
    Chill martini glass in freezer at least 10 minutes.
  2. 2
    Fill shaker with ice. Add Tito's, lemon juice, and rosemary simple syrup.
  3. 3
    Shake hard for 15 seconds until ice cold.
  4. 4
    Double strain into chilled martini glass.
  5. 5
    Garnish with rosemary sprig and lemon twist. Serve immediately.

Always serve up in a chilled martini glass — this cocktail deserves the elegance.

The rosemary aroma hits before the first sip — that's the magic.

Rosemary syrup recipe in the House Syrups tab. Keeps 2 weeks refrigerated.

Blood Orange Margarita
Tequila
Bold citrus and a Brandt's Bar signature twist
Ingredients
  • 3 ozHouse Tequila Blanco
  • 1¼ ozFresh lime juice
  • 1 ozFresh blood orange juice
  • ½ ozCointreau
  • 1Dried blood orange wheel, garnish
  • 1Fresh basil sprig, garnish
  • —Kosher salt for half rim
Method
  1. 1
    Run lime wedge around half the rim. Dip into kosher salt. Fill with ice.
  2. 2
    Add tequila, lime, blood orange juice, and Cointreau to shaker with ice.
  3. 3
    Shake hard 15 seconds.
  4. 4
    Strain over fresh ice in prepared glass.
  5. 5
    Perch dried blood orange wheel on rim. Tuck in fresh basil sprig.

Always use fresh squeezed lime and blood orange — bottled juice flattens the flavor.

Blood oranges most available November through March — note seasonally.

Half salt rim lets guests choose their experience.

Mai Tai
Rum · Summer
A tropical classic with Appleton Estate Jamaican Rum
Ingredients
  • 3 ozAppleton Estate Jamaican Rum
  • ¾ ozFresh lime juice
  • ½ ozCointreau
  • ¼ ozOrgeat Syrup
  • ¼ ozSimple Syrup
  • 1Fresh mint sprig, garnish
  • 1Lime wheel, garnish
Method
  1. 1
    Fill rocks glass or tiki mug with crushed ice.
  2. 2
    Add rum, lime, Cointreau, orgeat, and simple syrup to shaker with ice.
  3. 3
    Shake hard 15 seconds.
  4. 4
    Strain over fresh crushed ice.
  5. 5
    Slap mint sprig between palms to release aroma. Tuck alongside lime wheel.

Appleton Estate is the backbone — don't substitute.

Orgeat is the secret weapon — that almond sweetness is what makes it a Mai Tai.

Optional: float a small splash of dark rum on top before garnishing for a showstopper.

Blueberry Bourbon Smash
Bourbon
Bold, fruity, and refreshing — a perfect summer bourbon cocktail
Ingredients
  • 3 ozWoodford Reserve Bourbon
  • ¾ ozFresh lemon juice
  • ¾ ozSimple Syrup (adjust to taste)
  • 5Fresh mint leaves
  • 1 handfulFresh blueberries (+ extra for garnish)
Method
  1. 1
    Add blueberries and mint to shaker. Muddle gently until blueberries release juice — about 5 seconds.
    Don't over-muddle or it turns bitter.
  2. 2
    Add Woodford, lemon juice, and simple syrup. Fill with ice.
  3. 3
    Shake hard 15 seconds.
  4. 4
    Double strain over fresh ice in large rocks glass.
    Double straining removes skins and mint bits for a cleaner look.
  5. 5
    Garnish with blueberries on a cocktail pick and fresh mint sprig.

Simple syrup: ½ oz for drier, ¾ oz for sweeter — adjust to your crowd.

Woodford's vanilla and oak notes pair beautifully with blueberry.

Christmas Pomegranate Martini
Vodka · Christmas Tradition
The Brandt's Bar Christmas tradition — stunning, festive, always requested
Single Drink
  • 2 ozTito's Vodka
  • 1 ozPAMA Pomegranate Liqueur
  • 1 ozCointreau
  • ½ ozFresh lime juice
  • 1 ozPomegranate juice
  • 1Lime wheel, garnish
  • 1 tspPomegranate seeds, garnish
Christmas Batch — Serves 12
  • 3 cupsTito's Vodka
  • 1½ cupsPAMA Pomegranate Liqueur
  • 1½ cupsCointreau
  • ⅔ cupFresh lime juice
  • 1½ cupsPomegranate juice
Method
  1. 1
    Batch: Combine all in pitcher. Refrigerate up to 24 hours. Shake individual portions with ice and strain to order.
  2. 2
    Single: Shake all ingredients with ice 15 seconds. Strain into chilled martini glass.
  3. 3
    Garnish with lime wheel on rim and float pomegranate seeds on top.

PAMA is what makes this special — don't substitute.

Pomegranate seeds floating in the glass look stunning at Christmas.

Red sanding sugar rim makes it extra festive.

Batch flavors meld beautifully overnight — make ahead!

Whiskey Lemonade
Bourbon · Summer Batch
Fresh squeezed lemonade meets Woodford Reserve — a summer crowd pleaser
Batch Ingredients — 750ml bottle yields ~12 drinks at 4oz each
  • 750 mlWoodford Reserve Bourbon (full bottle)
  • 7 ozFresh squeezed lemon juice
  • 4 ozSimple syrup
  • 6–8 dashesOrange bitters
  • TopLemon bubbly water (per drink)
  • 1Lemon wheel, garnish
Method
  1. 1
    Combine Woodford, lemon juice, simple syrup, and orange bitters in a large pitcher. Stir well. Refrigerate until service.
  2. 2
    To serve: pour 4oz of batch over ice in a highball glass.
  3. 3
    Top with lemon bubbly water. Light stir.
  4. 4
    Garnish with lemon wheel. Serve immediately.

Always use fresh squeezed lemon — bottled juice kills this drink.

Orange bitters are the secret ingredient — don't skip them.

Batch can be made up to 24 hours ahead. Add bubbly water per drink at service only.

4oz per drink is the Brandt's Bar standard pour from this batch.

The Caipirinha
Cachaça · Summer
Brazil's national cocktail — muddled lime, sugar, and cachaça over ice
Ingredients
  • 3 ozCachaça (Brazilian sugarcane spirit)
  • 1Whole lime, cut into chunks
  • 2 tspWhite sugar
  • 1 cupCrushed ice
Method
  1. 1
    Cut lime into chunks and place directly in a rocks glass. Add sugar.
  2. 2
    Muddle firmly to release the lime juice and dissolve the sugar into the juice.
    Muddle well — you want all the juice and oils out of the lime.
  3. 3
    Fill the glass with crushed ice.
  4. 4
    Pour cachaça over the ice. Stir well to combine. Serve as-is with the muddled lime in the glass.

Cachaça is made from fresh sugarcane juice — not rum. They're different spirits.

Leblon or Novo Fogo are excellent widely available cachaça brands.

The lime chunks stay in the glass — that's traditional and correct.

Built directly in the glass — no shaker needed.

Freezer Door Old Fashioned
Bourbon · Make-Ahead Batch
A full bottle of Woodford transformed into instant Old Fashioneds — always ready in the freezer
Batch Ingredients — yields ~17 drinks
  • 750 mlWoodford Reserve Bourbon (full bottle)
  • ½ ozAngostura Bitters
  • ½ ozOrange Bitters
  • 4 ozSimple Syrup
  • 6 ozWater
  • 1Large ice cube per serving
  • 1Orange peel per serving, garnish
Method
  1. 1
    Combine Woodford, both bitters, simple syrup, and water in a large pitcher or directly back into the empty bourbon bottle. Stir well.
  2. 2
    Pour into a sealable container or back into the bourbon bottle. Place in freezer. The alcohol content keeps it from fully freezing.
  3. 3
    To serve: pour approximately 3oz over a large single ice cube in a rocks glass.
    No stirring needed — it's already diluted and chilled perfectly.
  4. 4
    Express orange peel over the glass, run around the rim, and drop in. Serve immediately.

The water is pre-dilution — it replicates the dilution from stirring so the drink is perfectly balanced straight from the freezer.

Keeps in the freezer indefinitely — perfect for spontaneous guests.

Both Angostura AND orange bitters gives this more complexity than a standard Old Fashioned.

Always express the orange peel — the oils make the drink.

Peach Bourbon Smash
Bourbon · Summer Seasonal
Fresh peaches meet Woodford Reserve — the taste of summer in a glass
Ingredients — Muddle these together
  • 3 ozWoodford Reserve Bourbon
  • 4Large fresh mint leaves
  • ½ ozSimple syrup
  • ¼Lemon, cut into chunks
  • 3Fresh peach slices
  • 1Large single ice cube
  • 1Fresh mint sprig, garnish
  • 1Fresh peach slice, garnish
Method
  1. 1
    Add mint leaves, simple syrup, lemon chunks, and peach slices to a shaker. Muddle firmly until peach and lemon release their juice and mint is fragrant.
    Muddling the lemon chunks gets the oils from the peel — more flavor than juice alone.
  2. 2
    Add Woodford Reserve and fill shaker with ice.
  3. 3
    Shake hard for 15 seconds.
  4. 4
    Double strain over a large single ice cube in a rocks glass.
    Double straining keeps it clean and elegant — removes all peach skin and mint bits.
  5. 5
    Garnish with fresh mint sprig and a fresh peach slice on the rim. Serve immediately.

Only make this when peaches are in season — fresh ripe peaches make all the difference.

The large ice cube is key — keeps it cold without diluting the fresh fruit flavors.

Woodford's vanilla and caramel notes pair beautifully with fresh peach.

A seasonal Brandt's Bar specialty — enjoy it while it lasts! 🍑

The Brandt's Mule
Bourbon · Vodka · Tequila — Your Choice
Pick your spirit, pick your mood — three classic mules, one perfect recipe
Ingredients
  • 3 oz🥃 Woodford Reserve — Kentucky Mule
    🫙 Tito's Vodka — Moscow Mule
    🌵 House Tequila — Mexican Mule
  • ¾ ozFresh lime juice
  • 4 ozFever-Tree Premium Ginger Beer
  • 1Fresh mint sprig, garnish
  • 1Lime wedge, garnish
  • 1 cupIce (crushed preferred)
Method
  1. 1
    Choose your spirit:
    🥃 Woodford Reserve = Kentucky Mule
    🫙 Tito's Vodka = Moscow Mule
    🌵 House Tequila = Mexican Mule
  2. 2
    Fill a copper mug or rocks glass with ice.
  3. 3
    Add spirit of choice and fresh lime juice.
  4. 4
    Gently pour in Fever-Tree Ginger Beer. Light stir to combine — don't lose the fizz.
  5. 5
    Slap mint sprig between palms to release aroma. Tuck alongside lime wedge. Serve immediately.

🥃 Kentucky Mule — Woodford's vanilla and caramel warm up the ginger beautifully

🫙 Moscow Mule — The classic. Clean, crisp, and refreshing

🌵 Mexican Mule — Tequila's earthy agave plays perfectly with the spicy ginger

Copper mug is traditional and keeps the drink ice cold longer.

Always slap the mint — the aroma is half the experience.

Brandt's House Margarita
Tequila
Steven's recipe — the foundation of the Brandt's Bar tequila program · 2:1:1 ratio
Ingredients
  • 3 ozHouse Tequila Blanco
  • 1½ ozFresh lime juice
  • 1 ozSimple syrup
  • ½ ozCointreau
  • —Kosher salt for rim (optional)
  • 1Lime wedge, garnish
The House Ratio
  • 2 partsTequila
  • 1 partSweet (simple syrup + Cointreau)
  • 1 partSour (lime juice)
Method
  1. 1
    Salt the rim if desired — run lime wedge around rim and dip in kosher salt.
  2. 2
    Add tequila, lime juice, simple syrup, and Cointreau to shaker filled with ice.
  3. 3
    Shake hard for 15 seconds.
  4. 4
    Strain over fresh ice in a rocks glass or serve up.
  5. 5
    Garnish with lime wedge. Serve immediately.

Steven's recipe — the 2:1:1 ratio is the key. Scale up or down but never change the ratio.

Always fresh squeezed lime — never bottled.

For the Blood Orange version see the Blood Orange Margarita recipe.

House tequila TBD — tasting between Espolòn, Olmeca Altos, and Cimarron.

Black Walnut Manhattan
Rye Whiskey · Winter Staple
A Brandt's Bar winter tradition — rich, complex, and unforgettable
Single Serving
  • 3 ozBulleit Rye Whiskey
  • ½ ozAverna Amaro
  • ½ ozCarpano Antica (sweet vermouth)
  • 1 tspNocino (black walnut liqueur)
  • 1 dashAngostura Bitters
  • 1Orange peel, garnish
Batch — 10 Servings
  • 30 ozBulleit Rye Whiskey
  • 5 ozAverna Amaro
  • 5 ozCarpano Antica (sweet vermouth)
  • 1½ ozNocino (black walnut liqueur)
  • 10 dashesAngostura Bitters
  • 1Orange peel per glass, garnish
Method
  1. 1
    Chill a coupe or martini glass in the freezer at least 10 minutes.
  2. 2
    Add ice to mixing glass. Pour in Bulleit Rye, Averna, Carpano Antica, Nocino, and Angostura bitters.
  3. 3
    Stir slowly and deliberately for 30 seconds. Do NOT shake.
    Stirring keeps it crystal clear and perfectly diluted.
  4. 4
    Strain into chilled coupe glass.
  5. 5
    Express orange peel over the glass, run around the rim, drop it in. Serve immediately.
    The orange oils balance the walnut perfectly.

Nocino is potent — a small teaspoon is all you need. Don't over-pour.

Carpano Antica is non-negotiable here — it's the richest sweet vermouth available.

Averna adds a bitter herbal complexity that makes this more than just a Manhattan.

A Brandt's Bar winter staple — written on a guest check and earned its place on the menu. 🌰

🌶️ Coriander Red Pepper Syrup
Used in: The Old Fashioned (Daily)
Ingredients
  • 1 cupWater
  • 1 cupWhite sugar
  • 2 tbspWhole coriander seeds
  • GenerousRed pepper flakes — to taste
Method
  1. 1
    Toast coriander seeds in dry pot over medium heat 2–3 min until fragrant.
  2. 2
    Remove from heat. Crack seeds with muddler right in the pot.
  3. 3
    Return to heat. Add water, sugar, and generous red pepper flakes. Stir until dissolved.
  4. 4
    Steep on low. Taste with a dipped spoon every few minutes. When you feel the heat on your palate — it's ready.
    Usually 15–30 minutes depending on your red pepper.
  5. 5
    Strain through fine mesh into sealed jar. Cool and refrigerate. Keeps 2 weeks.
🌿 Rosemary Simple Syrup
Used in: Marilyn's Rosemary Lemon Martini
Ingredients
  • 1 cupWater
  • 1 cupWhite sugar
  • 4 sprigsFresh rosemary (6-inch)
Method
  1. 1
    Combine water and sugar over medium heat. Stir until dissolved.
  2. 2
    Add rosemary sprigs. Remove from heat.
  3. 3
    Steep until fully cooled — about 30 minutes. Longer = stronger flavor.
    Taste as you go.
  4. 4
    Strain into sealed jar or squeeze bottle. Refrigerate. Keeps 2 weeks.
🍊 Grapefruit Shrub
Used in: Bourbon.Bacon.Beautiful · Start 3 days ahead · Keeps 3 months
Ingredients
  • 1 LFresh grapefruit juice
  • 1 LWhite sugar
  • ½ LWhite wine vinegar
  • ½ LApple cider vinegar
Method
  1. 1
    Day 3: Combine grapefruit juice and sugar. Simmer until dissolved. Do not boil. Cool completely. Refrigerate 24 hours.
  2. 2
    Day 2: Add both vinegars. Stir well. Bring to gentle simmer, remove from heat immediately.
  3. 3
    Cool completely. Label, date, refrigerate. Keeps 3 months.
The Bourbon.Bacon.Beautiful requires 3 days of prep. Here's your complete timeline. 🥓🥃
Day 3 — Start Here
Make the Grapefruit Shrub Base
Combine 1L grapefruit juice + 1L sugar in pot. Simmer until dissolved. Do NOT boil. Cool completely. Refrigerate 24 hours. Do not add vinegar yet.
Day 2 — Morning
Finish the Grapefruit Shrub
Add ½L white wine vinegar + ½L apple cider vinegar to chilled syrup. Bring to gentle simmer, remove from heat immediately. Cool, label, date, refrigerate.
Day 2 — Afternoon
Start Bacon Fat Wash
Bake 1lb bacon at 325°F for ~10 min. Stripe with maple syrup. Return to oven 2–3 min until caramelized. Reserve liquid bacon fat. Pour 4 cups fat into Woodford Reserve. Stir well. Rest at room temp 4 hours.
Day 2 — Evening
Freeze the Bourbon
Place fat-washed bourbon in freezer overnight until bacon fat solidifies completely on top.
Day 1 — Morning
Skim & Strain the Bourbon
Skim solidified bacon fat from top. Strain bourbon through fine mesh or cheesecloth into clean container. Refrigerate until service.
Day of Service
Make the Candied Bacon Garnish
Use reserved maple-glazed bacon from Day 2. Break or cut into 3–4" strips. Store in airtight container until service. You're ready — let's go! 🥓
